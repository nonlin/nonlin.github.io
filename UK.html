Simple fairy cakes

Ingredients

  • 2 eggs
  • 4oz Self Raising
  • 4oz Margarine
  • 4oz Caster Sugar

PROTIP: If you want to make these cakes chocolatey, replace 1oz of self-raising flour with coco powder. This can be special cocoa powder or simply hot chocolate powder.

Method

1)      Place the margarine and the sieved sugar in a mixing bowl and cream together until pale and fluffy.

2)      Add one egg and a tablespoon of the 4ox of flour. Mix in.

3)      Repeat step two but mixing for slightly longer until all is well combined.

4)      Sieve the rest of the flour into the mixture and fold in.

5)      Place 12 bun cases into a muffin tin and divide the mixture between the 12 cases.

·         PROTIP: Fill the case about 1/2  - 2/3 of the way up. This will give the cake room to rise and grow!

6)      Place in the oven for 15 minutes at 180˚c – until golden brown.

·         PROTIP: you can get a cake tester or simply a knife for this. Stick a knife into the centre of the cake and pull it out. If the knife or tester comes out clean (with no cake mix on it), then the cakes are done.

7)      Leave to cool before icing

ButterCREAM for Fairy Cakes

Ingredients

  • 50g Margarine
  • 100g Icing Sugar

PROTIP: If you want chocolate buttercream, add some coco powder into the mixture along with the icing sugar. Add to taste.

Method

1)      Place the margarine into a bowl and soften by mixing it.

2)      Sieve in the icing sugar and mix well.

·         PROTIP: Go slow at first because the icing sugar will go everywhere!

3)      If the mixture is too stiff, then add a splash of milk.

4)      You can either spoon the mixture directly onto each cake or use an icing bag to pipe the cream onto each cake.

 

Giant Yorkshire Pudding

Ingredients

  • 2 Eggs
  • 4 tablespoons of plain flour
  • A pinch of salt and pepper
  • Semi-skimmed milk
  • Water

Method

1)      Sieve the flour into a bowl and add the salt and pepper. Mix together.

2)      Add the 2 eggs and add enough milk so that it just covers the flour. Mix this together thoroughly until everything is combined.

3)      Add a splash of water and whisk again. Set aside to rest while you complete the next steps.

4)      Grease a small casserole dish with oil and put this in the oven to heat up for 5 mins.

5)      Take out the oil. Whisk the batter mixture once more and pour the mix into the casserole dish.

6)      Place in the oven for 25 – 30 minutes at 200˚c. The Yorkshires should have risen and should be golden brown.

·         PROTIP: you can get a cake tester or simply a knife for this. Stick a knife into the centre of the Yorkshire and pull it out. If the knife or tester comes out clean (with no batter mix on it), then the Yorkshires are done.

7)      Serve immediately and enjoy!

Indulgent Gluten Free Chocolate Cookies

Ingredients

·         200g softened butter

·         300g soft brown sugar

·         2 tsp vanilla extract

·         2 eggs

·         300g Gluten Free self raising flour

·         80g cocoa powder

·         1 tsp Gluten Free baking powder

·         300g Chocolate of your choice, I usually go for 100g each of white, milk and dark

Method

1.    Preheat oven to 180 c and place grease proof paper on a baking tray

2.    Cream butter and sugar until light and fluffy, then beat in vanilla extract and eggs

3.    In a separate bowl sift flour, cocoa and baking powder together

4.    Add flour mix to the creamed mix in stages( a third at a time) mix well.

5.    Chop chocolate up into small chunks and fold into mixture until just combined.

6.    Dollop the mixture on to a baking tray with a tablespoon until you have used up all the cookie mixture.

7.    Bake in preheated oven for 12 minutes, when you remove the cookies from the oven they will still be soft, leave to cool completely on the baking tray, where they will continue to cook slightly.

8.    When completely cold move to a cooling rack.

Marshmallow Muffins

Ingredients

·         2 cups flour

·         3 teaspoons baking powder

·         ½ cup cocoa powder

·         ½ cup sugar

·         ¼ cup miniature marshmallows

·         ½ cup milk chocolate chips

·         1¼ cups milk

·         5 tablespoons butter, melted

·         1 egg

Method

Preheat oven to 375.

In a large bowl, combine the milk, butter, and egg. Add the flour, baking powder, cocoa powder, sugar, marshmallows, and chocolate chips, and stir until just mixed; do not over stir. Spoon the batter into greased or papered muffin tins, filling each cup two-thirds full. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Cool for 10 minutes in the tin, then transfer to a wire rack and cool completely. Serve immediately or store in an airtight container for up to 1 week.
