George Erfesoglou - Software Engineering | Engine Integrations | Connected Devices (BLE/Serial)

Platform and engine-integration engineer building developer-facing SDKs and plugins for real-time 3D applications. Focus areas are Unity and Unreal native interop, cross-platform BLE/serial transports, and reliability-focused device messaging.

Core Expertise

  • Unity (C#/.NET) and Unreal Engine (UE4/UE5, C++/Blueprint) SDK/plugin development for connected devices
  • Cross-platform delivery across Win64, Android, iOS, macOS, and Linux (Raspberry Pi)
  • Interop and native systems: P/Invoke, C ABI design, Win32 serial overlapped I/O, WinRT, CoreBluetooth, JNI
  • Transport and lifecycle architecture: scan/discovery, permission gating, connect/disconnect, reconnection, teardown safety
  • Messaging and diagnostics: BLE GATT, serial protocols, WebSockets, framing/parsing, retries/timeouts, UDP telemetry
  • Engine tooling and packaging: Slate/Editor tooling and Unreal Build.cs packaging wiring

Selected Work

  • Owned end-to-end Unity and Unreal integrations for a BLE-controlled hardware device from transport layers and SDK APIs to release-ready builds.
  • Shipped a cross-platform Unreal plugin across Win64, macOS, Android, and iOS with native BLE backends and build/packaging wiring.
  • Architected a unified cross-platform SDK exposed through Unity (C#) and Unreal (C++) bindings with compile-time guarded backends.
  • Built OVRSerialWin.dll in C++ and integrated it into Unity via P/Invoke for IL2CPP/.NET Standard compatibility.
  • Engineered lifecycle reliability and diagnostics for scan/connect/reconnect and thread-safe callback dispatch.
  • Developed a UE4 simulation library of 10+ NOV equipment/components for pre-commissioning control-logic validation.
  • Built a Fabric 1.20.4 mod with a WebSocket bridge for gameplay-driven scent/visualization orchestration.
  • Published Physical Boids on Fab/Unreal Marketplace.
